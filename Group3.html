What’s a Robo-Advisor?

Robo-advisors offer low-cost, portfolio suggestions , prioritizing long-term profitability and tailored financial goals.

Different Types Of Robo-Advisor

Type1: Motif

Motif is a investment platform providing investors able to invest in U.S. listed stocks, ETFs, and IPO.

Instead of investing in some separated product, traders can invest in a specific theme which is a package up to 30 products sharing a common concept.

AI application

Trading Algorithm Create themes for users based on individuals’ previous investment or their propensity
Big Data Analysis Collect datas to analyze some vital indicators, e.g. "Top Performers in 1 Year Return”

Watchlist Algorithm Recommends the latest related news and information to the users according to watchlist
Auto-investment Invest in themes itself and alter the strategy by analyzing the situation of the market

Type2: Wealthfront

Using the service Wealthfront provided, investors will first asked to fill out a questionnaire,

then Wealthfront could build a diversified portfolio tailored for the investors based on their risk tolerance and objectives.

Customers will only need to choose from the portfolio recommended, then Wealthfront will take the rest —

reinvesting dividends, rebalancing the portfolio in a tax-efficient way, and performing daily automated Tax-Loss Harvesting.

AI application

Portfolio Construction Wealthfront determines the optimal mix of our chosen asset classes by using the concept of MPT and asks AI to collect historical data and calculate the volatility of the targets.

With the volatility, customers’ risk tolerance and objectives and some other indexes, Wealthfront could give them a collection of portfolios that maximize expected return for each level of risk.
Rebalance Algorithm To maintain the intended risk level and asset allocations, a portfolio must be periodically rebalanced, so AI monitors the investors’ portfolios and periodically rebalances each portfolio. Using the “Rebalancing Algorithms” trade off deviations from the target portfolio with the tax consequences of selling appreciated assets, AI use cash inflows to buy underweight asset classes in an effort to reduce turnover, taxes, and trading costs.

Operational Phase

1. Investment Goal Setting Define clear objectives to tailor financial advice.
2. Risk Assessment determine risk level through targeted questions.
3. Financial Planning Utilize Monte Carlo simulations to calculate goal achievement probabilities, offering financial planning advice.
4. Investment Advice Provide Investment Portfolio Recommendations based on risk profile and goals.
5. Monitoring Management Regularly monitor investment portfolios, maintaining allocation ratios through rebalancing.
